SDGs in Higher Education Context

Click to view larger version

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), adopted by the United Nations in 2015, provide a universal framework for addressing global challenges. In higher education, academic literacy development becomes a critical mechanism for translating these global goals into local educational practices and research.

Academic literacy enables students to engage with complex global challenges through critical analysis, research skills, and effective communication.

Multimodal Academic Literacy

Click to view larger version

Academic literacy extends beyond language alone; multimodality reveals how meaning is constructed through multiple semiotic resources. In academic settings, visual elements (layout, typography), aural features (tone, pace), gestures, and spatial arrangements all contribute to communication.

Meaning is constructed through the integration of multiple semiotic resources, each contributing uniquely to the overall message in academic communication.

Genre and Mode Switching in Academic Literacy

Click to view larger version

Within academic contexts, students navigate vertical knowledge structures—such as essays, presentations, and notes—each with genre-specific conventions. These vertical structures are complemented by horizontal mode shifts: from internal thought to spoken discussion, written notes to visual representations.

Students must develop awareness of genre and mode-specific qualities when navigating academic communication contexts.

Conclusion

The synergy between academic literacy development and the UN Sustainable Development Goals is undeniable. Higher education plays a pivotal role in cultivating the intellectual capacities and critical skills necessary for individuals to contribute meaningfully to global sustainability efforts. By prioritizing inclusive and effective academic literacy programs, educational institutions can empower a new generation of leaders and innovators to tackle the world's most pressing challenges and realize the vision of the SDGs.
